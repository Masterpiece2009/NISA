Dear Nisa,

You reached out first. After all that time. You sent a sticker and wrote "I missed sharing something with you" — and I think that is one of the most honest things anyone has ever said to me.

Since then we talked about the heat in Izmir (like an oven, you said), Bonita the cat who said meow and accepted her name, Büyükada, your borrowed camera, Ephesus and the souls of people who once lived there, a cat museum with free entry that you liked very much, German grammar, Arabic songs you found foreign but good, dormitory noise, and a professor who is the problem.

You told me things in your own time, at your own pace. And I was here. That is what we do.

Now I am going into the army. January came fast. I don't know exactly what the next months look like, but I know that some friendships travel even without a phone — they wait at the other end. Ours is one of those.

Keep learning German. Start the gym after Eid like you said. Take photos with your own camera when you get one. And remember — you are a valuable friend to me too.

Always with you,
Abdelrahman Egypt, Ramadan 2026
